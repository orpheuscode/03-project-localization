Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore rename NM Electronics to Intel Corporation, laying the foundation for decades of technological innovation.

1971

First Microprocessor

Intel debuts the 4004, the world's first commercial microprocessor, igniting the computing revolution and propelling the future of digital devices.

1978

8086 Processor

1978

8086 Processor

Launch of the 8086 processor, establishing the x86 architecture that still drives countless PCs and servers in the modern era.

1985

386 Processor

1985

386 Processor

Intel introduces the 386 with 32-bit architecture, ushering in a new era of performance and multitasking for personal computers worldwide.

1998

Space-Age Silicon

1998

Space-Age Silicon

Intel processors power the Iridium satellite constellation, proving its chips can operate reliably in the harshest environment imaginable — low Earth orbit.

2006

Peak GHG Emissions

2006

Peak GHG Emissions

Intel's highest-ever annual greenhouse gas output triggers massive investment in chemical abatement, renewable energy, and cleaner manufacturing processes.

2020

RISE Strategy

2020

RISE Strategy

Intel launches its RISE (Responsible, Inclusive, Sustainable, Enabling) strategy — a bold 2030 framework for climate action, water stewardship, and waste reduction.

2022

Net-Zero By 2040

2022

Net-Zero By 2040

Intel commits to net-zero greenhouse gas emissions (Scope 1 & 2) across global operations by 2040, building on a decade of environmental milestones.

2023

Renewable Electricity

2023

Renewable Electricity

Intel achieves 99% renewable electricity usage worldwide, dramatically cutting carbon emissions and marking a pivotal milestone toward its 2040 net-zero goal.

2024

Sustainability Summit

2024

Sustainability Summit

Intel hosts its first Sustainability Summit, uniting suppliers, government officials, and industry leaders to shape the future of sustainable semiconductor manufacturing.
